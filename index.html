Ways to prepare your home before a vacation

Vacation time is upon us! For many of us, that means packing up the car and hitting the open road. But before you embark on your journey, there are a few things you should do to prepare your home. When leaving, you should always think about the other things you did not prepared or settled prior to leaving.

  1. Unplug all unnecessary electronics
  2. Turn off the water to your home (or at least to any appliances that use water) to avoid any leaks while you’re gone.
  3. Make sure all perishable food is either eaten or thrown out
  4. Let a trusted neighbor know you’ll be gone and ask them to keep an eye on your place
  5. Make sure all doors and windows are locked
  6. Set your alarm
  7. Put a hold on your mail or have a friend or neighbor pick it up for you while you’re away
  8. If you have pets, make arrangements for someone to take care of them while you’re gone
  9. Disconnect any automatic payment services that could overdraft your account while you’re away
  10. Double check that everything is turned off and locked up before leaving on vacation!

By taking these simple steps, you can rest assured knowing that your home is safe and sound while you’re away enjoying your well-deserved vacation. Now all that’s left to do is sit back, relax, and enjoy the ride!

Make sure all appliances are turned off and unplugged

In the event of a power outage, it is important to make sure all appliances are turned off and unplugged. This includes things like your oven, stovetop, fridge, and even your computer. If these things are left on, they could be damaged when the power comes back on. In some cases, they could even cause a fire. So, it’s better to be safe and just turn everything off. If you have any battery-operated devices, like a flashlight, make sure to keep them handy in case you need them.

If you’ll leave your house for a long period of time like a vacation trip, it’s important to unplug all appliances as well. This is because if there is a power surge, it could damage your appliances or even start a fire. So, it’s always better to be safe and just turn everything off.

It’s also a good idea to disconnect any electronics that are not being used. For example, if you have a printer that is not in use, you should unplug it. This way, if there is a power surge, it will not damage your electronics.

In general, it’s always best to err on the side of caution when it comes to electricity. If you’re not sure whether or not you should unplug something, it’s better to do so just to be safe. Better safe than sorry!

Hide your car key in a designated spot outside your home

Once you’ve chosen a spot, make sure to always put your car key back in the same place. That way, you’ll always know where to find it when you need it.
